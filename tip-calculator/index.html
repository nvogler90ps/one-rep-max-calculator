Tipping FAQs

How much should you tip at a restaurant?

In the United States, the standard tip at a restaurant is 15-20% of the pre-tax bill. For excellent service, 20-25% is common. For counter service or takeout, 10-15% is typical. Always tip on the pre-tax amount, not the total after tax.

How do you calculate a tip?

To calculate a tip, multiply the bill amount by the tip percentage as a decimal. For example, a 20% tip on a $50 bill: $50 x 0.20 = $10.00 tip. The total bill would be $50 + $10 = $60. To split, divide the total by the number of people.

How do you split a bill with tip?

To split a bill with tip: first calculate the tip on the full bill amount, add it to the bill to get the total, then divide the total by the number of people. For example, a $100 bill with 20% tip ($20) totals $120 -- split 4 ways, each person pays $30.

Do you tip on tax?

Etiquette experts recommend tipping on the pre-tax subtotal, not the after-tax total. The tax goes to the government, not the server. However, tipping on the full total including tax is also acceptable and makes the math simpler -- either approach is fine.
