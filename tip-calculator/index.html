Tipping FAQs

How much should I tip at a restaurant?

In the United States, the standard tip at a restaurant is 15-20% of the pre-tax bill. For excellent service, 20-25% is common. For counter service or takeout, 10-15% is typical. Always tip on the pre-tax amount, not the total after tax.

How do you calculate a 20% tip?

To calculate a 20% tip, multiply the bill amount by 0.20. For example, 20% of a $50 bill is $50 x 0.20 = $10.00. A quick shortcut: move the decimal point one place left to get 10%, then double it. So $50 becomes $5.00, doubled to $10.00.

What is the formula for calculating a tip?

The tip formula is: Tip = Bill Amount x (Tip Percentage / 100). For example, an 18% tip on a $75 bill: $75 x 0.18 = $13.50. To get the total, add the tip to the bill: $75 + $13.50 = $88.50. To split, divide the total by the number of people.

Is 15% still a good tip?

A 15% tip is still acceptable for adequate service at a sit-down restaurant, though 18-20% has become the new standard in most of the US. For excellent service, 20-25% is expected. A 15% tip may be more appropriate for counter service, buffets, or takeout orders.

How do I split a bill with tip?

To split a bill with tip: first calculate the tip on the full bill amount, add it to the bill to get the total, then divide the total by the number of people. For example, a $100 bill with 20% tip ($20) totals $120 -- split 4 ways, each person pays $30.
