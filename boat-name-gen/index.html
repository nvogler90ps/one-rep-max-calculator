Frequently Asked Questions

How do I name my boat?

Choosing a boat name is a personal tradition. Consider what the boat means to you -- an escape, an adventure, a joke. Use this generator to brainstorm ideas, then pick the one that makes you smile every time you see it on the transom. Keep it short enough to fit the stern, and make sure it sounds good spoken over the radio.

What are the most popular boat names?

Perennial favorites include Serenity, Island Time, Nauti Girl, Seas the Day, Reel Therapy, and Second Wind. Puns dominate the top lists year after year, with fishing-themed names especially popular among smaller boats. Yacht owners tend toward more elegant names like Endeavour or Sapphire.

Is it bad luck to rename a boat?

Maritime superstition says yes -- renaming a boat without a proper ceremony angers Poseidon. The traditional denaming ceremony involves purging every trace of the old name, making an offering to the sea gods, and christening the boat with its new name. Many owners rename boats with no issues, but the ceremony is a fun tradition and a great excuse for a dock party.

How do I register a boat name?

In the US, boat registration is handled by your state's DMV or Department of Natural Resources. The boat name itself is not formally registered at the state level -- only documented vessels (registered with the US Coast Guard) have their names checked for uniqueness. Coast Guard documentation is optional but required for vessels over 5 net tons used in commercial service or traveling internationally.
